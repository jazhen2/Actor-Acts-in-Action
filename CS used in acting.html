Computer science with acting

Human-computer interaction, and computer-supported collaborative work, the series aims to bring together and mutually inform comceptual devlopments and empirical explorations of the technological mediation of human activity in these and other fields such as sociology, communication, education, and organizational studies.


Acting with technology volumes encompass a diversity of theoretical frameworks including activity theory, actor network theory, distributed cognition, and other practice-based theories developed through enthnomethodological and grounded theory approaches.
